How it works

From submitted to under contract, on rails.

  1. 1

    Wholesalers submit deals

    Address, contract proof, ARV support, photos, rehab notes, deadlines, and access — all in one structured submission. No more "DM me for info."

  2. 2

    Each deal gets a tier

    Submitted, Contract verified, or Reviewed Deal Packet. The tier tells buyers exactly what's been checked before they spend an hour underwriting.

  3. 3

    Verified buyers act

    Buyers filter by area, strategy, and budget. They review the packet, save the deal, and submit interest privately. The wholesaler sees who's serious.

Verification tiers

You always know what's been checked.

Submitted

Not independently reviewed

Submitted by a wholesaler but not yet vetted. Numbers and claims come from the submitter. Treat like any other off-market lead — do your own due diligence.

Contract verified

Contract or marketing rights confirmed

We've confirmed the submitter has a signed contract, JV agreement, or written marketing authorization for the property. Cuts the daisy-chain problem at the source.

Reviewed packet

Comps, rehab, and risks reviewed

Our team reviews comps, ARV range, rehab estimate, rental potential, and red flags. The deal arrives with a full packet so a serious buyer can decide in minutes.

Deal packet anatomy

Everything you need to underwrite in one place.

A Reviewed Deal Packet is structured the same way every time — so you stop hunting for missing info.

1. Snapshot

  • Address & area tag
  • Deal type (assignment / double close / JV)
  • Buyer price, ARV range, rehab range, spread
  • Closing & inspection deadlines
  • Occupancy & access
  • Verification tier

2. Investment fit

  • Best-fit strategy (flip, BRRRR, rental, STR, land hold)
  • Skill level needed (cosmetic vs full rehab)
  • Capital required (cash-only or HML friendly)
  • Expected hold or exit timeline

3. Numbers & comps

  • Recent comparable sales (with addresses)
  • Active and pending comps
  • Rehab line items by category
  • Rent estimate & pro forma (for rentals)
  • Subject-to / seller-finance terms (if applicable)

4. Risks & red flags

  • Foundation, roof, electrical, plumbing notes
  • Flood zone status
  • Title or probate concerns
  • Tenant or occupancy issues
  • Short closing or assignability restrictions

5. Documents

  • Redacted contract or marketing rights
  • Photos (interior, exterior, problem areas)
  • Disclosures from seller (if available)
  • Title status / preliminary title (if opened)
